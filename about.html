About Alexander

A calm, thoughtful space to make sense of things.

I am interested in how people come to understand themselves, their relationships, their patterns, and the choices available to them.

I am Alexander, a counselling trainee offering a steady, reflective space for adults who want to talk things through carefully and honestly.

My route into counselling has been shaped by a long-standing interest in psychology, identity, relationships, language, and the way people make meaning from their lives. Before training as a counsellor, I worked across marketing, writing, websites and personal identity work.

What matters in my work

I do not believe useful counselling has to feel complicated or full of jargon. Often, the work begins by slowing things down enough to notice what has happened, how it has affected you, and what you need now.

I am especially interested in helping people separate what they know from what they fear, understand their capacity, and find small, honest next steps that feel possible rather than forced.

A secure way of relating

The thread running through my work is simple: helping people relate to themselves, other people and uncertainty with more clarity, steadiness and self-trust.

That does not mean pretending things are easy. It means creating enough safety and space to look at what is really there without attacking yourself for being human.
