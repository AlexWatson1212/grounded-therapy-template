Counselling in Stockport and online

A steadier place to understand yourself.

I'm Alexander, a counselling trainee working with adults who want a calm, thoughtful space to make sense of what they are carrying.

You may not need fixing. You may need space to understand.

People often come to counselling when life has become difficult to hold alone. Sometimes there is a clear reason. Sometimes there is only a sense that something feels stuck, heavy or hard to explain.

Anxiety and overthinking: when your mind keeps checking, analysing or preparing for what might go wrong.

Low confidence and self-doubt: when you find yourself questioning your worth, choices or place in the world.

Relationships: when old patterns, boundaries, conflict or loss are difficult to make sense of.

Change and uncertainty: when a new chapter of life asks more of you than you expected.

Neurodivergent experience: when you want to understand your needs, capacity and ways of relating more clearly.

Counselling does not need to begin with perfect words. Bringing the muddle is enough.

The clarity loop.

A simple thread runs through my work: when life feels confusing, it helps to slow down the process between what happened and what we decide to do next.

Observe: what actually happened?

Separate: what is evidence, and what is interpretation?

Regulate: what is my capacity today?

Decide: what is the smallest honest next step?

Reflect: what did I learn from what happened next?

This is not about positive thinking. It is about accurate thinking, self-trust and a more secure way of relating to yourself and the world.

A little about me.

I came to counselling through a long-standing interest in psychology, self-understanding and the ways people make sense of their lives.

Before training as a counsellor, I worked across marketing, writing and personal identity work. That background still shapes how I think: I am interested in the stories people carry, the patterns they repeat, and the language they use to understand themselves.

My approach is thoughtful, steady and grounded in the belief that people often need less pressure and more honest space to hear themselves clearly.
